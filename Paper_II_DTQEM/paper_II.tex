\begin{abstract}
Building on Paper~I, where a single effective phonon mode with wavenumber $\tilde{\nu}=711\pm48\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ provided an accurate effective description of the temperature-dependent zero-field splitting $D(T)$ of NV centers, we extend the DTQEM framework to spin-lattice relaxation $T_1(T)$ and propose a corresponding fluctuation ansatz for pure dephasing. We demonstrate that the fixed-frequency single-mode Raman factor $n(\omega,T)[n(\omega,T)+1]$ is mathematically bounded by a $T^2$ high-temperature asymptote and cannot reproduce the $T^5$-like Raman scaling observed experimentally over intermediate temperature ranges in NV centers. Replacing the discrete mode with an effective Debye--Raman transport integral $J_5(\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}/T)$ provides a physically interpretable continuum description of this scaling and yields a consistent effective cutoff $\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}=596\pm88\,\mathrm{K}$ across three Jarmola ensemble samples. The identity $\coth(x)=2n(x)+1$ provides a unified thermal language linking static shifts, fluctuation-driven dephasing ansatzes, and multi-phonon relaxation within a single phonon-occupation framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The nitrogen-vacancy (NV) center in diamond is a leading platform for quantum sensing, with applications ranging from nanoscale thermometry to single-spin magnetic resonance~\cite{Degen2017,Barry2020}. The performance of NV-based sensors is fundamentally limited by spin relaxation and decoherence, both of which are strongly temperature-dependent~\cite{Jarmola2012,Norambuena2018}. The temperature dependence of the zero-field splitting and its relation to phonons, thermal expansion, and spin-phonon coupling have been discussed in several theoretical and experimental works~\cite{Doherty2014,Cambria2024}.

In Paper~I of this series~\cite{Berramdane2026a}, we introduced the Decoherence-Temperature Quantum Effective Model (DTQEM) and showed that a single effective phonon occupation model accurately captures $D(T)$. Within that effective description, $D(T)$ primarily reflects a static, thermally averaged lattice-displacement scale. The longitudinal spin relaxation time $T_1(T)$ and the pure dephasing rate $\Gamma_\phi(T)$ are different observables. They are governed by dynamical fluctuations of the phonon bath and are often described by separate empirical or semi-empirical laws. Here we provide a physically motivated effective framework that organizes $D(T)$, quantitatively models $T_1(T)$, and suggests a testable ansatz for $\Gamma_\phi(T)$ within a common phonon-occupation language.

The central statistical identity is
\begin{equation}
\coth(x)=\frac{2}{e^{2x}-1}+1=2n(\tilde{\nu},T)+1, \qquad x=\frac{hc\tilde{\nu}}{2k_{\mathrm B}T},
\label{eq:coth_identity}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
n(\tilde{\nu},T)= \frac{1}{\exp(hc\tilde{\nu}/k_{\mathrm B}T)-1}.
\end{equation}
Thus the same Bose--Einstein statistics may appear as $n$, $2n+1$, or $n(n+1)$ depending on whether the observable probes static shifts, fluctuations, or Raman absorption-emission processes.

\section{Theoretical Framework}
\label{sec:theory}

\subsection{Effective spin-phonon Hamiltonian}
The NV ground-state spin is an effective $S=1$ system. Since the zero-field splitting $D_0$ is conventionally expressed in frequency units, the corresponding energy Hamiltonian is written as
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\hat H = hD_0\hat S_z^2 + \hbar\omega a^\dagger a + \lambda(a+a^\dagger)^2\hat S_z^2
} .
\label{eq:effective_hamiltonian}
\end{equation}
Here $a$ and $a^\dagger$ are phonon annihilation and creation operators, $\omega=2\pi c\tilde{\nu}$, and $\lambda$ is an effective quadratic spin-strain coupling energy. This model retains the leading quadratic sensitivity of the zero-field splitting to local strain. Macroscopic thermal expansion, anharmonicity, and orbital electron-phonon effects are absorbed phenomenologically into the fitted parameters.

\subsection{From $n(\tilde{\nu},T)$ to $\coth(\tilde{\nu},T)$}
For an effective phonon mode of wavenumber $\tilde{\nu}$, the Bose--Einstein occupation number is
\begin{equation}
n(\tilde{\nu},T)=
\frac{1}{\exp(hc\tilde{\nu}/k_{\mathrm B}T)-1}.
\label{eq:bose_occupation}
\end{equation}
If
\begin{equation}
\hat u=u_0(a+a^\dagger), \qquad u_0=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2M\omega}},
\end{equation}
then the thermal mean-square displacement is
\begin{equation}
\langle \hat u^2\rangle_T = u_0^2[2n(\tilde{\nu},T)+1] = u_0^2 \coth\!\left(
\frac{hc\tilde{\nu}}{2k_{\mathrm B}T}
\right).
\label{eq:mean_square_displacement}
\end{equation}
Since the zero-point contribution is temperature-independent, it is absorbed into the redefined constant $D_0'$. For the static zero-field splitting shift we thus write
\begin{equation}
D(T)=D_0'+A\,n(\tilde{\nu},T).
\label{eq:D_model}
\end{equation}
This is the single-mode occupation model used in Paper~I. For pure dephasing, the same occupation language suggests the effective fluctuation ansatz
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_\phi(T) = \Gamma_{\mathrm{bg}} + \alpha \coth\!\left(
\frac{hc\tilde{\nu}_\phi}{2k_{\mathrm B}T}
\right).
\label{eq:dephasing_ansatz}
\end{equation}
Here $\Gamma_{\mathrm{bg}}$ summarizes non-phononic background noise, including impurities, the $^{13}$C nuclear spin bath, strain, electric-field noise, and technical noise, while $\alpha$ is an effective phonon-mediated dephasing amplitude.

\subsection{Origin and $T^2$ ceiling of the single-mode Raman rate}
A two-phonon Raman process can arise either from second-order perturbation theory in a linear spin-phonon coupling or from an effective quadratic bath operator
\begin{equation}
\hat B\propto(a+a^\dagger)^2.
\end{equation}
In a single-mode Raman approximation, the absorption-emission thermal factor has the form
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_R^{\mathrm{single}}(T) \propto n(\omega,T)[n(\omega,T)+1].
\label{eq:single_mode_raman}
\end{equation}
Let
\begin{equation}
x=\frac{\hbar\omega}{k_{\mathrm B}T}.
\end{equation}
For $x\ll1$,
\begin{equation}
n(x) = \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{2} + \frac{x}{12} + \mathcal O(x^3),
\end{equation}
and therefore
\begin{equation}
n(n+1) = \frac{e^x}{(e^x-1)^2} = \frac{1}{x^2} - \frac{1}{12} + \mathcal O(x^2).
\label{eq:nnplusone_expansion}
\end{equation}
The leading high-temperature algebraic growth is therefore $T^2$. No higher positive power of $T$ can be generated within a fixed-frequency single-mode Raman ansatz with temperature-independent coupling.

\subsection{Effective Debye--Raman transport integral}
A more realistic Raman process involves pairs of phonons drawn from a continuum. Microscopically,
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_R \propto \int d\omega\, J(\omega)J(\omega+\omega_s) n(\omega)[n(\omega+\omega_s)+1].
\label{eq:microscopic_raman}
\end{equation}
For NV centers, $\omega_s$ lies in the GHz range, while thermally relevant lattice modes are typically in the THz range. Therefore $\omega_s\ll\omega$, and one may approximate
\begin{equation}
J(\omega+\omega_s)\simeq J(\omega), \qquad n(\omega+\omega_s)\simeq n(\omega).
\end{equation}
This motivates the effective form
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_R(T) \propto \int_0^{\omega_c}d\omega\, W(\omega) n(\omega,T)[n(\omega,T)+1],
\label{eq:effective_raman_integral}
\end{equation}
where $W(\omega)$ is an effective two-phonon spectral weight. In the minimal Debye--Raman model we take
\begin{equation}
W(\omega)\propto\omega^4.
\label{eq:omega4_weight}
\end{equation}
Introducing
\begin{equation}
x=\frac{\hbar\omega}{k_{\mathrm B}T}, \qquad \Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}=\frac{\hbar\omega_c}{k_{\mathrm B}},
\end{equation}
one obtains
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_R(T) \propto T^5 \int_0^{\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}/T} x^4 \frac{e^x}{(e^x-1)^2} dx.
\end{equation}
We define
\begin{equation}
\boxed{ J_5(y) = \int_0^y x^4 \frac{e^x}{(e^x-1)^2} dx }, \qquad y=\frac{\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}}{T}.
\label{eq:J5_definition}
\end{equation}
The corresponding relaxation model is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\frac{1}{T_1(T)} = \Gamma_0 + R \left( \frac{T}{\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}} \right)^5 J_5\!\left( \frac{\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}}{T} \right)
} .
\label{eq:J5_model}
\end{equation}
The limiting forms are
\begin{equation}
J_5(y)\xrightarrow[y\to\infty]{} \frac{4\pi^4}{15}, \qquad J_5(y)\xrightarrow[y\to0]{} \frac{y^3}{3} + \mathcal O(y^5).
\label{eq:J5_limits}
\end{equation}
Thus Eq.~\eqref{eq:J5_model} has a $T^5$ low-temperature Debye--Raman asymptote and crosses over toward $T^2$ when $T\gtrsim\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}$.

The extracted $\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is an effective spectral cutoff of the relevant two-phonon channels and should not be identified with the bulk Debye temperature of diamond.

\subsection{Unified DTQEM picture}
The three phenomena considered here probe different aspects of the same phonon bath:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Phenomenon} & \textbf{Physical origin} & \textbf{Effective form} & \textbf{Status} \\
\midrule
$D(T)$ & Static shift & $D_0'+A n(\tilde{\nu},T)$ & Fitted in Paper~I \\
$\Gamma_\phi(T)$ & Fluctuation ansatz & $\Gamma_{\mathrm{bg}}+\alpha\coth[hc\tilde{\nu}_\phi/(2k_{\mathrm B}T)]$ & Testable ansatz \\
$1/T_1(T)$ & Raman transitions & $\Gamma_0+R(T/\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}})^5J_5(\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}/T)$ & Tested here \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Although these expressions share a common Bose--Einstein occupation language, the characteristic energy scales $\tilde{\nu}$, $\tilde{\nu}_\phi$, and $\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}$ need not coincide.

\section{Experimental Validation}
\subsection{Datasets and fitting procedure}
We test the transport integral model primarily on the Jarmola \textit{et al.} ensemble datasets and discuss Lin \textit{et al.} and Barbosa \textit{et al.} as additional consistency checks. Parameter uncertainties were evaluated using bootstrap resampling with $10^4$ iterations. Because the Jarmola data were digitized from published figures, the quoted uncertainties should be regarded as effective fit uncertainties rather than original experimental standard errors.

\section{Discussion}
\subsection{Why a single mode suffices for $D(T)$ but not for $T_1(T)$}
Within the effective description of Paper~I, $D(T)$ is represented by a static thermal displacement scale. Such a thermal average can be captured by a single effective phonon energy. In contrast, $T_1(T)$ is a dynamic transition rate. Raman relaxation requires phonon pairs satisfying $|\omega_1-\omega_2|\approx\omega_s$. A single perfectly sharp mode cannot provide a continuum of near-degenerate phonon pairs with the required frequency differences.

\subsection{Why $J_5$ succeeds physically despite similar $R^2$}
The value of the $J_5$ model is not primarily that it yields a much larger $R^2$. Rather, its advantage is physical interpretability. The preference for $J_5$ is therefore physical: the single-mode fit reproduces the data only by assigning an effective frequency above the one-phonon diamond optical phonon scale, while $J_5$ explicitly represents the continuum character of a two-phonon Raman process and yields a physically interpretable continuum cutoff.

\subsection{Limitations and outlook}
The present framework is intentionally effective. It should be viewed as a compact intermediate description between purely empirical power laws and full \textit{ab initio} spin-phonon theory. Its main limitations are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The $J_5$ model uses a minimal $\omega^4$ spectral weight.
\item The pure dephasing expression in Eq.~\eqref{eq:dephasing_ansatz} remains a testable ansatz.
\item The extracted $\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}$ may include effective contributions from Raman, local-mode, and Orbach-like processes over the fitted window.
\item Digitization of published figures introduces uncertainties; fits to raw experimental data would reduce these uncertainties.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}
We have extended the DTQEM phonon-occupation framework from the static zero-field splitting $D(T)$ to spin-lattice relaxation and proposed a corresponding fluctuation ansatz for pure dephasing. The single-mode picture that succeeds for the static shift fails as a physical description of Raman relaxation because the factor $n(n+1)$ has a $T^2$ high-temperature ceiling within the fixed-frequency single-mode Raman ansatz. Replacing the discrete mode by an effective Debye--Raman transport integral yields a physically interpretable continuum model with $\Theta_{\mathrm{eff}}=596\pm88$ K across three Jarmola ensemble samples. The present work is not a substitute for microscopic spin-phonon theory; it offers a compact, organized bridge between effective models and experimental data, and suggests testable forms for future dephasing experiments.
