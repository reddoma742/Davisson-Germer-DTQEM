\begin{letter}{The Editor}

\opening{Dear Editor,}

I am pleased to submit, as an independent researcher, the manuscript
entitled \textbf{``A Single-Mode Occupation-Number Model for
Temperature-Dependent Zero-Field Splitting in NV Centers''} for
consideration in your journal.

The temperature dependence $D(T)$ of the zero-field splitting in
NV centers underpins quantum thermometry and precision sensing, yet
current descriptions rely on empirical polynomial fits with limited
physical interpretability. This work addresses that gap with a
parsimonious, physically motivated model. The key contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The model achieves $R^2 = 0.999946$ on the high-precision
    Ouyang et al.\ dataset, matching the best published polynomial
    within experimental uncertainty while retaining direct physical
    meaning via three interpretable parameters.

    \item The extracted effective phonon frequency
    $\omega = 711 \pm 48$\,cm$^{-1}$ is consistent with the diamond
    phonon spectrum, providing a physically meaningful energy scale
    for the dominant spin--phonon coupling channel.

    \item \textbf{Most significantly}, when calibrated solely on
    298--383\,K, the model predicts $D(T)$ up to 1000\,K with
    $R^2 = 0.998185$ and a maximum deviation below 2\,MHz, while
    a power-law calibration fails outside the fitting window.
    Monte Carlo propagation of parameter uncertainties confirms
    the robustness of this extrapolation.
\end{enumerate}

This work provides a compact calibration tool for NV-based
nanothermometry and lays the groundwork for extending the
phonon-occupation framework to spin-lattice relaxation
$1/T_1(T)$ in a forthcoming companion paper.

I confirm that this manuscript has not been published elsewhere
and is not under consideration by another journal.
