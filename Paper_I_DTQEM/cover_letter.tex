\begin{letter}{Editor}

\opening{Dear Editor,}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled \textbf{``A Single-Mode Occupation-Number Model for Temperature-Dependent Zero-Field Splitting in NV Centers''} for publication.

This work presents a physically motivated model for $D(T)$ based on the Bose-Einstein occupation of a single effective phonon mode. The key contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The model achieves $R^2 = 0.999946$ on high-precision data, matching the best empirical polynomial to within experimental uncertainty while using the same number of parameters (3) and providing physical interpretability.
    
    \item The extracted effective phonon frequency $\omega = 711 \pm 48$\,cm$^{-1}$ is physically consistent with the diamond phonon spectrum.
    
    \item \textbf{Most significantly}, when calibrated solely on 298--383\,K, the model predicts $D(T)$ up to 1000\,K with $R^2 = 0.998185$ (maximum deviation $<2$\,MHz), while a power-law calibration fails outside the fitting window.
\end{enumerate}

We believe this work will be of interest to the quantum sensing community.

\closing{Sincerely,}
