## 6. Discussion & Open Questions

- The balance condition \(V = D\) provides a clean quantitative test for pure dephasing environments. We invite experimental groups to test this prediction using e.g. superconducting qubits or trapped ions with controlled dephasing.
- In the tunneling and Schottky models, complementarity \(V^2 + D^2\) can drop far below 1 due to mixed-state dynamics. This is not an error but a feature of open quantum systems.
- The exploratory codes in `experiments/` (negative tunneling time, observer frequency) are **hypothetical** and not yet experimentally validated. They are intended to stimulate theoretical discussion and may lead to new predictions if refined.
- Future work includes:
  - Extending the balance condition to non‑Markovian environments.
  - Including the effect of finite temperature on the balance condition (beyond pure dephasing).
  - Designing a realistic double‑slit experiment that can reach \(V = D\) using the derived relation.

## 7. Conclusion

DTQEM provides a unified, numerically exact framework for simulating open quantum systems under the Time‑Sovereignty interpretation. Three concrete physical applications (Davisson–Germer, double‑well tunneling, Schottky effect) have been implemented, tested, and documented. The discovery of an **exact analytical balance condition** \(V = D\) for pure dephasing elevates the model from a numerical tool to a predictive theory. The condition corrects earlier misconceptions and opens the door to quantitative experimental tests.
